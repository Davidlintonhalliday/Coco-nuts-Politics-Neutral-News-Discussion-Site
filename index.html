How preferential voting works (Australia)

Australia uses instant‑runoff voting (IRV) for the House of Representatives and STV for the Senate. This explainer outlines ballots, preferences, and quotas in plain English.

Read overview
  1. Lower House (HoR): Rank candidates 1, 2, 3… If no candidate gets 50%+, the lowest is eliminated and preferences are redistributed until someone wins a majority.
  2. Upper House (Senate): Proportional representation using single transferable vote (STV) with quotas; you can vote above or below the line.
  3. Why it matters: Encourages minor‑party participation and reduces “spoiler” effects.

Media literacy basics

  • Check the date and original source.
  • Read beyond headlines; examine evidence.
  • Distinguish news, analysis, and opinion.
  • Beware of cherry‑picked stats and cropped clips.
  • Balance your feed with credible counter‑views.

About & editorial standards

Mission

We curate across reputable outlets and add context with explainers. We separate news, analysis, and opinion, and disclose sources.

  • Accuracy over speed; corrections welcomed.
  • Transparent sourcing, link to originals.
  • No ad‑tech, minimal tracking.
  • Respectful speech; no harassment or slurs.

Bias & balance

We label leaning where relevant to help triangulation. Balance means steel‑manning major viewpoints, not equal airtime for fringe ideas.
